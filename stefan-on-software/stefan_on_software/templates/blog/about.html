About Stefan Kussmaul

Hey! My name's Stefan. I'm a recent graduate of the University of Massachusetts Amherst, and am now getting my Master's degree in Germany. I've always enjoyed writing and coding, so starting a blog was the natural thing to do! The name "Stefan on Software" comes from one of my favorite blogs, Joel On Software. I strive to produce high-quality content and hope to be as good as Joel some day.

This blog isn't just about software, though. I love travelling, and I've posted multiple articles about my travels. My parents both come from Europe (my mom from Poland, my dad from Germany) so I have a good excuse to spend time there. In 2018 I studied abroad at the University of Uppsala in Sweden, and wrote a bunch of articles about my time there.

I've been coding since I was 15 years old, when my dad forced persuaded me to take an intro C++ programming course. I was really bad at it--I didn't grasp the concepts naturally, and found it very frustrating. I felt like my brain just wasn't "wired" for coding. Since then, I've come to realize that this is the natural state of things--most people aren't "good" at anything until they put a lot of hard work into it. But it does feel like there's a common stereotype that all programmers just naturally knew how to code. In any case, I'm glad I learned the way I did, because I now have a special level of empathy for those who are new to programming.

Over the past few years, software has been my main hobby, and is now my profession. There's nothing more satisfying than designing and building a complicated project, and I have notebooks full of sketches and specifications I created while brainstorming one project or another. I think coding is an intensely creative activity, and it gives individuals the ability to do super cool things with just their computer and their brain.

My other hobbies include tennis and basketball. Between work, exercise, and personal software projects I don't have a lot of time for video games, but when I do, I play Super Smash or Rome Total War. My favorite TV shows are Arrested Development, Curb Your Enthusiasm, and The Office.

Feel free to contact me on LinkedIn, or check out my Github.
