About Stefan Kussmaul

I am a 24-year old software engineer from the US, currently living in Switzerland and working at Google. I got my Bachelor's in Computer Science at UMass Amherst and my Master's in Software Engineering at the Technical University of Munich.

I've been coding since I was 15 years old, when my dad forced persuaded me to take an introductory C++ programming class. I didn't like it—I felt like my brain just wasn't "wired" for coding.

What saved the day was my discovery of Minecraft mods. At the time, people would take the Minecraft source code and add their own code to it. There were some amazing mods—my favorite was one which added industrial machines and factories to the game.

I really wanted to write my own mod, but I would need to become a good programmer first. I began coming up with little programming projects to work on in my spare time, and gradually I started to like it.

I started this blog in 2017 to experiment with Python Flask. Since then I've spent many hundreds of hours writing posts and improving the code. The name "Stefan on Software" comes from one of my favorite blogs, Joel On Software. I strive to produce high-quality content and hope to be as good as Joel some day.

This blog isn't just about software, though. I love traveling, and I regularly write posts about my life abroad. I also like to do small data analysis projects using Python. With the rest of my free time, I play tennis and explore new places in Switzerland. My favorite TV show is Curb Your Enthusiasm, and my favorite video games are Minecraft and Rome Total War.

Feel free to reach out on LinkedIn, or check out my Github.
