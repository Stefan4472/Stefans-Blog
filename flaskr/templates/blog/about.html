My name's Stefan. I'm an undergrad studying computer science at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst. I've always enjoyed writing and coding, so starting a blog (and coding the website) was the natural thing to do! The name "Stefan on Software" comes from one of my favorite blogs called Joel On Software. It's a fantastic blog for software engineers, and I hope to someday produce content that's as good.

This blog isn't just about software, though. I love travelling, and I've posted multiple articles about my travel. Last year, I studied abroad at Uppsala University in Sweden, and you can find the blog articles about it as well.

I've been coding since 10th grade, when my dad convinced (forced?) me to take an intro C++ programming course. It was a long time before it started to grow on me--I didn't grasp the concepts naturally, and a lot of the time I felt like my brain just wasn't "wired" correctly for coding. Typing out code was pedantic, and one small mistake would break the entire program. What a struggle!

Luckily, I played a lot of video games, especially Minecraft. And that made me really, really want to write a Minecraft mod adding new items and features to the game. To do that I really, really needed to learn how to code, and that desire sustained me through the difficult ordeal of learning to code.

It also helped that my friend Matthew knew what he was doing, and was a patient teacher. Together we started a programming club (which eventually even grew to three people) and met every Friday to plan projects and discuss coding. We started a "company", PlainSimple apps, and wrote a few small programs. It was an incredibly good experience for me, and made me comfortable coding and using Github.

But that was all in highschool. In the intervening time, coding has become my main hobby, and something I love doing. There's nothing more satisfying than designing and building a complicated project, and I have notebooks full of sketches and API specifications I created while brainstorming one project or another. I think coding is an intensely creative activity, and I want to build cool things.

My other hobbies include tennis and basketball. Between school, sport, and personal projects I don't have a lot of time for video games, but when I do I play Super Smash or Rome Total War.

Feel free to contact me on LinkedIn, or check out my Github.
