About

Note: There is an ongoing issue where the links on this page aren't working correctly.

My name's Stefan. I'm an undergrad studying computer science at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst. I've always enjoyed writing and coding, so starting a blog (and coding the website) was the natural thing to do! The name "Stefan on Software" comes from one of my favorite blogs called Joel On Software. It's a fantastic blog for software engineers, and I hope to someday produce content that's just as good.

This blog isn't just about software, though. I love travelling, and I've posted multiple articles about my travels. My parents both come from Europe (my mom from Poland, my dad from Germany) so I have a good excuse to spend time there! I speak German, Polish, and French, which also makes travel easier. In 2018 I studied abroad at the University of Uppsala in Sweden, and wrote a bunch of articles about my time there.

I've been coding since 10th grade, when my dad forced persuaded me to take an intro C++ programming course. I was really bad at it--I didn't grasp the concepts naturally, and coding caused my brain to hurt. I felt like my brain just wasn't "wired" for coding. It was so frustrating!

Lucky for my dad, I played a lot of video games. One of my favorites was Minecraft, which had been initially written by a single dude who called himself "Notch." I had all these ideas for cool things to add to Minecraft, And that made me really, really want to write Minecraft mods. I realized that in order to do that I would have to get really good at coding, and that drive sustained me through the difficult ordeal of learning to code.

It also helped that my friend Matthew knew what he was doing, and was a patient teacher. Together we started a programming club (which eventually grew to *three* people) and met every Friday to plan projects and talk about coding. We started a "company" called PlainSimple Apps, which would publish beautiful, intuitive software. We wrote a few small programs, although we lacked the experience to produce anything amazing. Nevertheless it was an incredibly good experience for me, and made me comfortable coding and talking about code.

But that was all in highschool. Since then, coding has become a big hobby of mine, and something that I love to do. There's nothing more satisfying than designing and building a complicated project, and I have notebooks full of sketches and specifications I created while brainstorming one project or another. I think coding is an intensely creative activity, and it gives individuals the ability to do super cool things with just their computer and their brain.

My other hobbies include tennis and basketball. Between school, sport, and personal projects I don't have a lot of time for video games, but when I do I play Super Smash or Rome Total War. My favorite TV shows are Arrested Development, Curb Your Enthusiasm, Six Feet Under, and The Office (if that even needed mentioning!)

Feel free to contact me on LinkedIn, or check out my Github.
