About

Hey! My name's Stefan. I'm a recent graduate of the University of Massachusetts Amherst, where I studied Computer Science. I've always enjoyed writing and coding, so starting a blog was the natural thing to do! The name "Stefan on Software" comes from one of my favorite blogs called Joel On Software. It's a fantastic blog for software engineers, and I strive to one day produce content that's just as good.

This blog isn't just about software, though. I love travelling, and I've posted multiple articles about my travels. My parents both come from Europe (my mom from Poland, my dad from Germany) so I have a good excuse to spend time there! I speak German, Polish, and French, which also makes travel easier. In 2018 I studied abroad at the University of Uppsala in Sweden, and wrote a bunch of articles about my time there.

I've been coding since 10th grade, when my dad forced persuaded me to take an intro C++ programming course. I was really bad at it--I didn't grasp the concepts naturally, and coding literally made my brain hurt. I felt like my brain just wasn't "wired" for coding. It was so frustrating! Because of that, I have a special level of empathy for those who are new to programming.

Lucky for me, I played a lot of video games. One of my favorites was Minecraft, which had been initially written by a single guy who called himself "Notch." I had all these ideas for cool things to add to Minecraft, and that made me really, really want to get better at coding. That desire sustained me through the difficult ordeal of learning to code.

Since then, software has been my main hobby, and now my profession. There's nothing more satisfying than designing and building a complicated project, and I have notebooks full of sketches and specifications I created while brainstorming one project or another. I think coding is an intensely creative activity, and it gives individuals the ability to do super cool things with just their computer and their brain.

My other hobbies include tennis and basketball. Between work, exercise, and personal software projects I don't have a lot of time for video games, but when I do, I play Super Smash or Rome Total War. My favorite TV shows are Arrested Development, Curb Your Enthusiasm, Six Feet Under, and The Office (if that even needed mentioning!)

Feel free to contact me on LinkedIn, or check out my Github.
